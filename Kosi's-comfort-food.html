What do you know about African salad

African salad or Abacha as it should be known is one of the many pride and joys of the ndi igbo, living in the eastern part of Nigeria.What more could you ask of a meal that provides everything needed for a balanced diet and more. If it is not already obvious, I'm very passionate about it. So we'll be taking a deep dive into the culinary goodness that is Abacha.

Ingredients Amount
Dried cassava chips 6 to 8 cups
Ugba(Ukpaka) 2 cups
crayfish 1/2 cup
seasoning cubes 1 cube; add more according to preference
palm oil 150ml
salt and pepper to taste
garden eggs (optional) 3
Utazi leaves (optional) half a handful; smaller if possible
Garden egg leaves (optional) to taste
protein source;Meat/dry fish/fried fish/stock fish.(optional) as much as you want

So how do we go about making this dish? Glad you asked.

You can either heat or soak the dry Abacha in boiled water for about three minutes, then sieve and keep on a separate bowl.

Blend your crayfish and also slice the onions, garden egg leaves.

Grab the seperate bowl with the abacha and get ready to mix all the ingredients thoroughly

Add about 100ml of palm oil

Then add the ground crayfish and pepper

Add the ugba to the mixture and continue to mix.Be adding salt to taste

Stir the whole combination and you are almost through with the preparation part.

The Jara

The garden eggs, the leaves, and the onions are used to spice up or for decoration and not added during preparation but while dishing out.They sliced and kept aside in different plates or bowls, then added while the individual plates are dished out; this also goes for the meat or fish used.Serve with the meat and also add the garden eggs (sliced) and leaves to individual plate.I usually pair this with a chilled glass of palmwine
